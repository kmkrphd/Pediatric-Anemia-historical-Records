# Pediatric-Anemia-historical-Records
real pediatric cohorts

The dataset is structured to represent a prospective, multisite, real-time pediatric anemia surveillance and follow-up system involving participating hospitals and pediatric care centers. The dataset is prospectively collected from multiple participating pediatric hospitals and tertiary care centers using a standardized electronic data collection protocol. At each site, eligible children aged 6 months to 17 years are enrolled using a standardized study protocol and assigned a pseudonymized patient identifier, after which demographic information, hematological measurements, clinical symptoms, pallor assessments, nutritional history, prescribed treatment, dosage, adherence, and referral outcomes are recorded at baseline and successive follow-up visits. Laboratory values are transferred from hospital laboratory information systems or entered through validated electronic case-report forms, while treatment and outcome variables are updated by authorized clinical personnel at the point of care. Each hospital retains identifiable information locally, whereas only de-identified, schema-standardized records are synchronized with the central research platform. Automated range checks, mandatory-field validation, duplicate detection, timestamp verification, unit harmonization, and cross-visit consistency rules are applied during data entry to maintain data quality. The longitudinal records are linked by patient and visit identifiers, enabling PediaAnemiaFormer to process evolving hemoglobin, red-cell indices, ferritin, inflammatory markers, treatment exposure, adherence, and clinical outcomes across time. 

The dataset represents 100 children aged 6 months to 17 years, stratified into infant/toddler, preschool, school-age, and adolescent groups, with four sequential follow-up visits per child, yielding 400 time-linked clinical records. It contains demographic variables, visit chronology, serial hematological indicators including Hemoglobin, Mean Corpuscular Volume, Mean Corpuscular Hemoglobin, Red Cell Distribution Width, Ferritin, C-Reactive Protein, and Reticulocyte percentage, together with treatment type, dosage, adherence, nutritional intervention, deworming status, symptom and pallor scores, treatment response, hospitalization, referral, and final outcome. The repeated-measures structure supports temporal progression analysis, response forecasting, relapse-risk modeling, and causal treatment comparison within PediaAnemiaFormer, while the age-group field enables subgroup evaluation across developmental stages. 

All observations are time-stamped, quality-checked using standardized validation rules, de-identified through pseudonymization, and harmonized into a common data model before integration into a research repository. The longitudinal cohort supports temporal disease progression analysis, treatment-response prediction, relapse-risk assessment, and multicenter validation.
